## Core Frameworks

### 1. Influence: The Six Principles of Persuasion (Robert Cialdini)

**The Core Principle:**
> "Human behaviour is governed by six core principles of persuasion: reciprocity, commitment, social proof, authority, liking, and scarcity. The real value lies in studying the psychological research and real-world case studies behind each one."

**The Six Principles:**
1. **Reciprocity** - People feel obligated to return favors
2. **Commitment & Consistency** - People want their actions to align with their self-image
3. **Social Proof** - People follow the behavior of others
4. **Authority** - People defer to experts and figures of authority
5. **Liking** - People say yes to those they like
6. **Scarcity** - People value things more when they're rare

**Use when:** Writing copy, designing marketing materials, making sales calls, building partnerships, or analyzing why people make decisions

**Key Insight:**
> "The case studies are the curriculum; the six principles are just the index. Study the research, not just the labels."

---

### 2. Zero to One: Contrarian Business Thinking (Peter Thiel)

**The Core Principle:**
> "Every truly successful business is built on a secret — a contrarian belief about the world that most people either haven't noticed or actively disagree with."

**Key Concepts:**
- **0 to 1** = Creating something genuinely new
- **1 to n** = Copying what already exists
- **Competition** = Race to the bottom, destroys margins
- **Monopoly** = The only sustainable path, preserves margins
- **Definite optimism** = The future is built by people with visions

**Use when:** Defining product strategy, competitive positioning, partnership differentiation, or personal career planning

**Key Question:**
> "What important truth do very few people agree with you on?"

---

### 3. Great Leads: Copywriting Hooks (Masterson & Forde)

**The Core Principle:**
> "The opening of any piece of copy determines whether your reader continues or leaves. There are six structural lead types, each calibrated to a different level of audience awareness."

**The Six Lead Types:**
1. **Direct Offer** - For hot, aware audiences who know they want the solution
2. **Direct Offer with Bullets** - For aware audiences who need details
3. **Big Promise** - For semi-aware audiences who are interested but not committed
4. **Testimonial** - For aware audiences who need social proof
5. **Story** - For cold, unaware audiences who need engagement
6. **Secret/How-To** - For unaware audiences who want to learn something new

**Use when:** Writing landing pages, emails, LinkedIn posts, sales pitches, or any communication where attention is scarce

**Key Insight:**
> "Audience awareness level dictates lead type — not personal style or preference. Choosing the wrong lead for the wrong audience is the single most common reason good copy fails."

---

### 4. The Challenger Sale: Teach, Tailor, Take Control (Dixon & Adamson)

**The Core Principle:**
> "The highest-performing sales reps are not 'Relationship Builders' who accommodate the customer — they are 'Challengers' who teach, tailor, and take control of the conversation."

**The Challenger Framework:**
1. **Teach** - Offer a new perspective the customer hadn't considered
2. **Tailor** - Adapt your message to their specific situation
3. **Take Control** - Guide the conversation, don't just accommodate

**Key Insight:**
> "The best insight is one that reframes how the customer sees a problem they already have. Relationship-building alone ranks last in sales performance — trust must be paired with insight."

**Use when:** Partnership pitches, investor meetings, sales calls, or any situation where you need to persuade someone to take action

---

### 5. Way of the Wolf: Straight-Line Selling (Jordan Belfort)

**The Core Principle:**
> "Every sale travels along an invisible straight line from opening to close. The seller's job is to keep the conversation on that line — moving forward, building certainty, never letting it deviate into irrelevant territory."

**Three Pillars of Certainty:**
1. **The Product** - Certainty that the solution solves their problem
2. **The Company** - Certainty that the organization delivers on promises
3. **The Salesperson** - Certainty that you're trustworthy and competent

**Use when:** Onboarding flows, sales calls, investor pitches, or any situation where you need to move someone from uncertainty to action

**Key Insight:**
> "Every objection is a signal of insufficient certainty — not a 'no'. Tonality and body language communicate more than words; master them first."

---

### 6. Thinking, Fast and Slow: Cognitive Bias Awareness (Daniel Kahneman)

**The Core Principle:**
> "The human mind operates via two systems: System 1 (fast, intuitive, emotional) and System 2 (slow, deliberate, rational). Most decisions are made by System 1, often with biases baked in."

**Key Concepts:**
- **System 1** = Fast, automatic, emotional, subconscious
- **System 2** = Slow, deliberate, logical, conscious
- **Regression to the Mean** = Exceptional results revert to average over time
- **Availability Bias** = Recent/dramatic events feel more probable
- **Overconfidence** = Confidence is not competence

**The Most Underused Insight:**
> "Regression to the mean means praising high performance and punishing low performance both produce the illusion of causation when the real driver is random variation."

**Use when:** Analyzing trading performance, evaluating product success, making hiring decisions, or reviewing any outcome-based metrics

---

### 7. Who Not How: Delegation & Leverage (Sullivan & Hardy)

**The Core Principle:**
> "High achievers are not held back by lack of skills — they are held back by asking 'How do I do this?' instead of 'Who can do this for me?' Every time you ask 'how,' you commit your own time. Every time you ask 'who,' you multiply your capacity."

**Key Concepts:**
- **Unique Ability** = The narrow zone where your talent and passion overlap
- **Who Question** = "Who can do this for me?" instead of "How do I do this?"
- **Time** = The only non-renewable resource; protect it by building a network of Whos

**Use when:** Overwhelmed by tasks, scaling a business, feeling stuck, or realizing you're the bottleneck

**Key Insight:**
> "Finding the right Who is a skill in itself — it requires clarity on outcomes, not tasks. Time is the only non-renewable resource."

---

### 8. 10x Is Easier Than 2x: Exponential Thinking (Sullivan & Hardy)

**The Core Principle:**
> "Aiming for 2x improvement requires doing more of what you already do. Aiming for 10x forces you to completely rethink your approach, eliminate 80% of current activities, and focus obsessively on the 20% that can produce exponential results."

**The Paradox:**
- **2x thinking** = Keeps you trapped in your current identity
- **10x thinking** = Requires becoming a different person
- **The 80% eliminated** = Was necessary to see what the critical 20% is
- **10x commitment** = Filters decisions automatically

**Use when:** Setting growth targets, feeling stuck in incremental progress, or needing breakthrough results

**Key Insight:**
> "10x is more achievable than 2x because it demands transformation rather than incremental effort. If it doesn't move you toward 10x, it's a distraction."

---

### 9. The Gap and the Gain: Progress Mindset (Sullivan & Hardy)

**The Core Principle:**
> "There are two ways to measure progress: against the ideal (the 'Gap') or against where you started (the 'Gain'). Living in the Gap breeds anxiety and dissatisfaction. Living in the Gain fuels confidence and momentum."

**Two Mindsets:**
- **Gap Thinking** = Measuring yourself against an ideal you haven't reached
  - Result: Anxiety, ingratitude, chronic dissatisfaction
  - The ideal is a moving horizon — designed to be unreachable

- **Gain Thinking** = Measuring how far you've come
  - Result: Confidence, resilience, forward momentum
  - Not complacency; it's the psychological foundation for long-term ambition

**Use when:** Reviewing performance, feeling discouraged, or after any setback

**Key Insight:**
> "Celebrating wins — even small ones — is not self-indulgence; it is neurological programming for continued achievement. The ideal is designed to be unreachable."

---

### 10. Great Mental Models: Frameworks for Thinking (Shane Parrish)

**The Core Principle:**
> "Most poor decisions come from applying the wrong mental model to a situation. A mental model is a framework for thinking — borrowed from physics, biology, mathematics, economics, or philosophy — that helps you see what others miss."

**Powerful Mental Models:**

1. **Inversion** - Think backwards from failure
   > Instead of asking "How do I succeed?" ask "What would guarantee failure?" Then avoid those things.

2. **First-Principles** - Strip away assumptions
   > Don't inherit assumptions. Rebuild your reasoning from the ground up for any important decision.

3. **The Map is Not the Territory** - Your model ≠ reality
   > Your model of reality is not reality itself. Recognize when you're working with abstractions.

4. **Circle of Competence** - Know what you don't know
   > Knowing what you don't know is more valuable than knowing what you do. Trade only within it.

**Use when:** Making strategic decisions, solving complex problems, or analyzing why something failed

**Key Insight:**
> "Circle of competence: Knowing what you don't know is more valuable than knowing what you do. Expand it deliberately."

---

### 11. 101 Things I Learned in Architecture School: Visual Thinking (Frederick)

**The Core Principle:**
> "This book is a masterclass in visual thinking, design principles, and structured problem-solving. Its lessons — about negative space, hierarchy, proportion, the relationship between form and function — translate directly into how we communicate ideas, design products, and structure arguments."

**Key Design Principles:**

1. **Negative Space** - What's absent defines form as much as what's present
   - Applies to: Product design, copy, conversation

2. **Hierarchy** - Communicates priority without words
   - Without it, everything competes and nothing wins

3. **Form and Function** - They are inseparable
   - "Designing from the outside in" means starting with the user's experience, not the builder's convenience

**Use when:** Designing UI/UX, writing content, creating pitch decks, or structuring any communication

**Key Insight:**
> "Architecture school's secret is that drawing forces clarity. Spend time sketching ideas as diagrams rather than words."

---

### 12. Unreasonable Hospitality: Customer Experience Culture (Will Guidara)

**The Core Principle:**
> "Most businesses settle for 'reasonable' hospitality — meeting the stated need. Unreasonable hospitality means anticipating the unstated need and exceeding it in a way that feels personal, human, and slightly magical."

**The Key Insight:**
> "Excellence is table stakes; what creates loyalty is the unreasonable gesture that says 'I was paying attention to YOU specifically'."

**Key Concepts:**
- **Reasonable Hospitality** = Meeting the stated need
- **Unreasonable Hospitality** = Anticipating the unstated need and exceeding expectations
- **The Gap** = Between good and legendary is almost always one decision in the moment by someone who cared enough to go further

**Use when:** Designing onboarding, building community, customer support, or any customer touchpoint

**Key Insight:**
> "Hospitality is not a department — it is a culture. Every touchpoint either reinforces it or undermines it. Reasonable keeps users. Unreasonable creates advocates."

## Decision Trees

### Which Principle Should I Use?

```
What's the situation?
├─ Persuasion/Influence needed
│  ├─ Marketing copy → Influence (social proof, authority, scarcity)
│  ├─ Sales call → Way of the Wolf (build certainty) OR Challenger Sale (teach insight)
│  └─ Partnership → Challenger Sale (reframe their problem)
├─ Strategic thinking needed
│  ├─ Product strategy → Zero to One (find your secret)
│  ├─ Competitive analysis → Great Mental Models (inversion, first-principles)
│  └─ Growth planning → 10x Is Easier Than 2x (exponential vs. incremental)
├─ Personal effectiveness needed
│  ├─ Overwhelmed → Who Not How (delegate)
│  ├─ Stuck → 10x Is Easier Than 2x (transform)
│  └─ Discouraged → The Gap and the Gain (measure gains, not gaps)
├─ Communication needed
│  ├─ Writing copy → Great Leads (match lead type to audience awareness)
│  └─ Designing communication → 101 Things I Learned in Architecture School (hierarchy, negative space)
└─ Customer experience needed
   └─ User touchpoint → Unreasonable Hospitality (anticipate unstated needs)
```

### Should I Use 2x or 10x Thinking?

```
What's the goal?
├─ Small improvement needed → 2x thinking (incremental, efficient)
└─ Breakthrough needed → 10x thinking (transformative, requires identity shift)
    ├─ Can you eliminate 80% of activities?
    │  ├─ Yes → 10x is achievable
    │  └─ No → Start with 2x, then scale to 10x
    └─ Will the result fundamentally change the business?
       ├─ Yes → 10x
       └─ No → 2x
```

### How Do I Measure Progress Correctly?

```
Feeling stuck or discouraged?
├─ Measuring against ideal? → STOP. This is Gap thinking.
└─ Measuring against starting point? → Continue. This is Gain thinking.

Daily habit:
├─ Gap question: "Why didn't I achieve more?" → Dangerous
└─ Gain question: "What moved forward today?" → Build confidence
```

### Which Lead Type Should I Use?

```
How aware is the audience?
├─ Hot/Aware (know they want your solution) → Direct Offer
├─ Aware but needs details → Direct Offer with Bullets
├─ Semi-aware (interested but not committed) → Big Promise
├─ Aware but needs proof → Testimonial
├─ Cold/Unaware (no idea they have the problem) → Story OR Secret/How-To
└─ Don't know? → Ask yourself: "How much education do they need before they're ready to buy?"
```

### 🚀 Zero to One Quick Guide

**The Question:**
> "What important truth do very few people agree with you on?"

**Mindset:**
- Competition = Race to bottom
- Monopoly = Sustainable advantage
- 0 to 1 = Create something new
- 1 to n = Copy what exists

**Daily Habit:**
Journal one "unpopular truth" per week about your industry

### ✍️ Great Leads Quick Guide

**Match Lead to Awareness:**
- **Hot/Aware** → Direct Offer
- **Aware, needs details** → Direct Offer with Bullets
- **Semi-aware** → Big Promise
- **Aware, needs proof** → Testimonial
- **Cold/Unaware** → Story OR Secret/How-To

**Key Rule:**
Audience awareness dictates lead type — not your preference

---

### 💡 Challenger Sale Quick Guide

**Framework:**
1. **Teach** - New perspective on their problem
2. **Tailor** - Adapt to their situation
3. **Take Control** - Guide the conversation

**Opening Formula:**
"Most [industry/people] think [current belief], but actually [your insight]. Here's why: [evidence]."

**Daily Habit:**
Before any sales call, write one "insight" — something they likely don't know

---

### 📈 Way of the Wolf Quick Guide

**Three Certainties:**
1. **Product** - This solves your problem
2. **Company** - We deliver on promises
3. **You** - I'm trustworthy

**Key Insight:**
Every objection = insufficient certainty, not a "no"

**Audit:**
Map every onboarding/sales flow as a straight line — does every step build certainty?

---

### 🧠 Thinking Fast and Slow Quick Guide

**Two Systems:**
- **System 1** - Fast, automatic, emotional (90% of decisions)
- **System 2** - Slow, deliberate, rational (10% of decisions)

**Most Important Insight:**
> **Regression to the mean** - Exceptional results revert to average. Don't over-learn from outliers.

**Daily Habit:**
Keep a decision journal — before important decisions, write reasoning and confidence level

---

### 👥 Who Not How Quick Guide

**The Shift:**
"How do I do this?" → "Who can do this for me?"

**Unique Ability:**
Where your talent and passion overlap — do ONLY this

**Weekly Review:**
"What did I spend time on that someone else could have done?"

---

### 🎯 10x Is Easier Than 2x Quick Guide

**The Paradox:**
10x = Transformation (easier)
2x = Incremental effort (harder)

**The Audit:**
Ask of every task: "Does this exist in my 10x future?"
- If no → Eliminate it

**The Filter:**
"Does this move me toward 10x?"
- If no → It's a distraction

---

### 📊 The Gap and the Gain Quick Guide

**Two Mindsets:**
- **Gap** = Measuring against ideal → Anxiety, dissatisfaction
- **Gain** = Measuring from start → Confidence, momentum

**Daily Habit:**
End each day with 3 gains — things that moved forward

**Key Insight:**
The ideal is designed to be unreachable. Gains sustain long-term ambition.

### 🧩 Great Mental Models Quick Guide

**Four Powerful Models:**
1. **Inversion** - Think backwards from failure
2. **First-Principles** - Strip away assumptions
3. **Map ≠ Territory** - Recognize abstractions
4. **Circle of Competence** - Know what you don't know

**Weekly Habit:**
Pick one model per week, apply to one challenge, write one-paragraph analysis

### 🏨 Unreasonable Hospitality Quick Guide

**The Difference:**

**Key Insight:**

**Daily Practice:**

### Example 4: Trading Performance Review (Thinking Fast and Slow)

## Common Pitfalls

### ❌ Influence Pitfalls

**Using principles unethically:**
- Problem: Manipulating people into decisions that don't serve them
- Solution: Only use persuasion ethically — help others make decisions that genuinely serve them

**Memorizing labels, not studying research:**
- Problem: Knowing "social proof" but not understanding when/how it works
- Solution: Read the case studies — the studies are the curriculum, principles are just the index

---

### ❌ Zero to One Pitfalls

**Copying competitors:**
- Problem: Thinking "they're doing well, so I should too"
- Solution: Competition is a race to the bottom — find your secret, not someone else's

**Secret that's not actually contrarian:**
- Problem: "Muslims want halal investing" — everyone agrees with this
- Solution: The secret must be something few people agree with

---

### ❌ Great Leads Pitfalls

**Using favorite lead type regardless of audience:**
- Problem: Always using story leads even for hot prospects
- Solution: Match lead type to audience awareness, not your preference

**Trying to sell in the lead:**
- Problem: "This product does X, Y, Z, buy now!"
- Solution: The lead's job is to make the next sentence irresistible, not close the sale

---

### ❌ Challenger Sale Pitfalls

**Being a jerk:**
- Problem: "You're wrong, here's why..."
- Solution: Respectfully disrupt — "Most people think X, but actually Y..."

**No actual insight:**
- Problem: "We're the best platform because we're great"
- Solution: Must reframe how they see their problem

---

### ❌ Way of the Wolf Pitfalls

**Treating objections as "no":**
- Problem: "They said it's too expensive, move on"
- Solution: Objection = insufficient certainty, build more certainty

**Ignoring tonality:**
- Problem: Focus only on words, not how they're delivered
- Solution: Tonality and body language communicate more than words

---

### ❌ Thinking Fast and Slow Pitfalls

**Assuming every good result is skill:**
- Problem: "I made money, I'm a genius!"
- Solution: Check for regression to the mean — exceptional results often revert to average

**Trying to eliminate System 1:**
- Problem: "I must only use System 2"
- Solution: System 1 is efficient for 90% of decisions; recognize when System 2 is needed

---

### ❌ Who Not How Pitfalls

**Delegating without clarity:**
- Problem: "Here's a task for you" (no outcome specified)
- Solution: Finding the right Who requires clarity on outcomes, not tasks

**Thinking "I can't afford to hire":**
- Problem: Waiting until revenue to delegate
- Solution: Whos can be unpaid — community collaboration, partnerships, barter

---

### ❌ 10x Pitfalls

**Trying to do 10x more work:**
- Problem: "I'll work 10x harder!"
- Solution: 10x requires eliminating 80% of activities, not doing more

**10x goal without 10x strategy:**
- Problem: "I want 10x users" (but still doing 2x tactics)
- Solution: 10x requires transformation, not incremental effort

---

### ❌ The Gap and the Gain Pitfalls

**Measuring only gains (complacency):**
- Problem: "Everything is great!" (no improvement)
- Solution: Gains fuel confidence, not complacency — use confidence to aim higher

**Never measuring gaps (no aspiration):**
- Problem: "I'm satisfied with where I am"
- Solution: The ideal drives ambition; just don't live in it exclusively

---

### ❌ Mental Models Pitfalls

**Using wrong model for situation:**
- Problem: Applying circle of competence to something you need to learn
- Solution: Recognize which model fits the situation

**Memorizing without applying:**
- Problem: "I know inversion" but never using it
- Solution: Weekly habit: apply one model to one challenge

---

### ❌ Visual Hierarchy Pitfalls

**Everything is bold/large:**
- Problem: If everything is important, nothing is important
- Solution: One element must clearly dominate each screen

**No negative space:**
- Problem: Cluttered, hard to read
- Solution: Negative space defines form — use it to guide the eye

---

### ❌ Unreasonable Hospitality Pitfalls

**Doing too much (overwhelming):**
- Problem: Giving users things they don't want
- Solution: Anticipate unstated needs, not random surprises

**Inconsistent hospitality:**
- Problem: Amazing one time, terrible the next
- Solution: Hospitality is a culture, not a campaign
